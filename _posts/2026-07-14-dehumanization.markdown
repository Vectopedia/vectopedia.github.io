Have you ever wondered about the consequences of your online actions? Whether it's what you say, what you upload, or who your online persona is? If you can read this article, it's likely you have thought about that at some point. If you use the Internet, this article is probably for you. What is a persona? What is an online persona? How does it affect how we socialize on the Internet? Let's find out!

# What is a persona?
According to the psychological definition, a persona is an imagined entity as the "ideal self". Your physical self, on the other hand, which is made of your cells and thoughts, is called the "real self". Personas are required for socialization– without them, we would be as vulnerable as raw egg yolk out of its shell. Personas are also used to motivate ourselves to become our better selves and to reach closer to our expectations. Examples of personas are endless; they can be whatever you think you should ideally be. One famous example is a __fursona__, which is a FURry perSONA. Vectopedia Jadeline Skye is my own, which I use to project my best self onto this character. A persona can be very healthy to have; because it is a motive for a better self, it can significantly boost confidence, self-esteem, and mental health in general.

# What is an online persona?
An online persona is practically the same thing, but applied to the Internet. Instead of simply using actions and words, it uses digitalized data, which you can infinitely customize, including but is not limited to usernames and appearances. The huge (dis)advantage to online personas is that you can entirely hide yourself, leaving no trace of your real self, such as voice.

# How does it affect how we socialize on the Internet?
The Internet has been, and will always be, a digital realm of chaos. I mean, as of the day of writing this article, I literally found a dude who claimed that he was the grim reaper, a god, a king, and having a scythe in his pants, all simultaneously! (Though, I'm not at liberty to disclose his name for privacy.) In a world of anonymity, without real names nor voice, the situation becomes confusing. __We often forget that, no matter innocent or villain, all of the people behind the screen are just as human as we are.__ We all have our own flaws, quirks, and voice, and hiding it makes it almost impossible to uncover. For instance, as a furry, I literally don't have any feline features on my physical self! Yet, it's perfectly normal to many that it's not actually me. We've taken this (dis)advantage for granted, and I think we should take action.

# So how should we take action?
> Which is worse?  
> Explosive rage, or cold silence?  
> For me, it is silence.  
> Rage is a clear signal, with a clear interpretation. Silence can mean everything, and nothing.  
> Silence is a blind spot, a black hole into which we can project our worst nightmares…
>
> – Stephen McCranie, Space Boy

Silence is a powerful thing. We constantly hide our personal information, which isn't a bad thing but in fact is a good idea to do so, but it causes conflict. We're unable to see the intentions nor consequences of one's online actions, whether it's insults or compliments. So speak up. If you see something is suspicious, report to a trusted one as soon as possible. If it's targeted towards you, feel free to ignore them, or report as said. Even explosive rage can help you uncover the problems.

But until then, __remember that we are all flawed humans, and there's no changing that.__
